# AnalyticsForge — Business Findings

> 7 data-driven insights discovered through analysis of 100,000+ Olist Brazilian E-Commerce orders from 2016–2018.

---

## 1. Revenue grew 8x from late 2016 to peak in late 2017

Monthly revenue grew from ~R$354 in October 2016 to a peak of ~R$1.2M in November 2017 — an 8x increase in just over a year. Revenue stabilized between R$1.0M–R$1.2M throughout 2018 before a sharp drop in October 2018 suggesting the dataset is incomplete for that month.

---

## 2. São Paulo dominates — but concentration is a risk

São Paulo state accounts for the largest share of both orders and revenue by a significant margin. The top 3 states (SP, RJ, MG) together account for over 60% of all orders. This geographic concentration means platform performance is heavily dependent on a single region's economic conditions.

---

## 3. Credit card is overwhelmingly the preferred payment method

Over 70% of all orders are paid by credit card, with boleto (Brazilian bank slip) as a distant second. Debit card and voucher usage is minimal. This suggests the platform should prioritize credit card experience and installment plan features.

---

## 4. Late deliveries correlate strongly with lower review scores

Orders delivered more than 7 days late receive an average review score of under 2.5 out of 5, compared to 4.3 for on-time deliveries. This 40%+ drop in satisfaction shows that delivery performance is the single biggest driver of customer experience on the platform.

---

## 5. Health & beauty is the top revenue category

Health and beauty products generate the highest total revenue across all categories, followed by watches/gifts and bed/bath/table. These three categories alone account for a disproportionate share of platform GMV and should be prioritized for seller acquisition and marketing.

---

## 6. Heavier products cost significantly more to ship

Analysis of product weight vs freight cost shows a clear positive correlation — products over 10kg have average freight costs 3-4x higher than lightweight items. Categories like furniture and appliances have the highest freight costs, which may be suppressing demand in those categories.

---

## 7. Seller location impacts delivery speed

Sellers based in São Paulo state deliver orders approximately 2-3 days faster than the platform average. Sellers in the Northeast region (BA, CE, PE) have the longest average delivery times. This suggests that expanding the seller base in underserved regions could significantly improve delivery SLAs and customer satisfaction nationwide.

---

*Analysis based on 99,441 orders across 3,095 sellers and 99,441 customers from October 2016 to October 2018.*
